## Style Guidelines

### DO Use:
- ✅ Real electrical equipment (conduit, wire, panels, tools)
- ✅ Dark mode aesthetic (reflects actual use cases)
- ✅ Technical precision (blueprints, diagrams, measurements)
- ✅ Human element (hands, electricians at work, but not faces)
- ✅ Industrial materials (metal, copper, steel)
- ✅ Safety colors (orange, yellow for warnings/highlights)
- ✅ Modern typography (clean sans-serif)
- ✅ Negative space (clean, uncluttered)

### DON'T Use:
- ❌ Generic tech startup imagery (MacBooks in coffee shops)
- ❌ Stock photo clichés (people in suits pointing at charts)
- ❌ Lightning bolts or electricity sparks (too literal, unsafe)
- ❌ Bright white backgrounds (not job-site friendly)
- ❌ Cartoonish or playful styles (this is serious work)
- ❌ Overly complex or cluttered compositions
- ❌ Generic "technology" imagery without electrical context

## Brand Consistency Rules

**Every Image Must:**
1. Pass the "electrician test" - would a journeyman electrician see it and nod?
2. Avoid tech startup clichés - no exposed brick, Edison bulbs, or bean bags
3. Reflect real work environments - job sites are dirty, messy, industrial
4. Show technical competence - details matter (correct wire colors, proper tools)
5. Maintain dark aesthetic - matches the app UI and actual work conditions

**Color Usage:**
- Electric blue = primary brand color (logos, CTAs, highlights)
- Safety orange = secondary (accents, warnings, action items)
- Grays/blacks = backgrounds, supporting elements
- Accent colors = sparingly, for specific calculator features

**Typography Pairing:**
- Headlines: Bold sans-serif (Inter, SF Pro, Helvetica Neue)
- Body: Regular sans-serif, high contrast for dark mode
- Code/Technical: Monospace (JetBrains Mono, SF Mono)
